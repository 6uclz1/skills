# Arrangement Energy

Use this reference when expanding loops into sections or evaluating whether an arrangement develops clearly.

## Density Scale

- `0`: silence, reset, or transition-only moment.
- `1`: sparse intro or outro; one identity element plus context.
- `2`: groove established; rhythm and one support part.
- `3`: full groove minus hook; most rhythm and harmonic support present.
- `4`: hook/drop; foreground identity is active.
- `5`: maximum-density peak; use rarely and intentionally.

## Section Fields

Each complete section plan should include:

- `density`: 0-5.
- `foreground`: hook, lead, vocal-like chop, or sharp transient focus.
- `midground`: chords, stabs, arps, percussion, rhythmic support.
- `background`: bass, drones, pads, noise beds, slow motion.
- `move`: what is added, muted, thinned, or committed.
- `transition_event`: fill, riser, silence, pickup, crash, automation, or no event.

## 64-Bar Skeletons

Balanced electronic sketch:

```text
Intro 8 | Groove 8 | Build 8 | Hook 16 | Break 8 | Hook Variation 12 | Outro 4
Density 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 1 | 4-5 | 1
```

Club-functional sketch:

```text
DJ Intro 16 | Groove 16 | Peak 16 | Breakdown 8 | Return 16 | Outro 8
Density 1-2 | 3 | 4 | 1 | 4-5 | 1
```

Song-forward sketch:

```text
Intro 4 | A 8 | B 8 | Hook 16 | Break 8 | Hook 16 | Outro 4
Density 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 1 | 5 | 1
```

## Development Rules

- Build the maximal-density section first when stuck.
- Copy it across the timeline, then remove parts to create contrast.
- Use fills only at section boundaries or where a subtraction leaves a gap.
- Let one element carry identity in each section; do not make all layers foreground.
- Do not raise density every section. Energy should breathe.
