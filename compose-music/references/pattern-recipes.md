Use these as starting points. Convert them to the user's genre, tempo, key, and available instruments.

## Drum Notation

One bar of 4/4 as 16 steps:

```text
01 02 03 04 05 06 07 08 09 10 11 12 13 14 15 16
1  e  &  a  2  e  &  a  3  e  &  a  4  e  &  a
```

General MIDI starting pitches:

- Kick: `36`
- Snare: `38`
- Clap: `39`
- Closed hat: `42`
- Open hat: `46`
- Low tom or percussion: `45`
- Rim or click: `37`

## Drum Recipes

Four-on-the-floor:

```text
Kick:  X . . . X . . . X . . . X . . .
Clap:  . . . . X . . . . . . . X . . .
CHat:  X . X . X . X . X . X . X . X .
OHat:  . . X . . . X . . . X . . . X .
```

Backbeat groove:

```text
Kick:  X . . . . . X . X . . . . . X .
Snare: . . . . X . . . . . . . X . . .
CHat:  X . X . X . X . X . X . X . X .
```

Half-time electronic:

```text
Kick:  X . . . . . . X . . . . X . . .
Snare: . . . . . . . . X . . . . . . .
CHat:  X . X . X . X . X . X . X . X .
```

Two-bar variation rule:

- Bar 1 states the groove.
- Bar 2 changes one of kick, snare, hat density, or final two steps.
- Use a short fill on steps `15-16` only when entering a new section.

Hat rule:

- Closed and open hats should not imply impossible simultaneous playing unless the sound design intentionally layers samples.

## Harmony Recipes

Pop lift:

```text
Roman: I | V | vi | IV
Use: major hooks, bright emotional release
Variation: start on vi for a minor-feeling loop
```

Minor anthem:

```text
Roman: i | VI | III | VII
Use: melodic electronic, synthwave, trance-leaning hooks
Variation: hold VII for two bars before returning to i
```

Dorian vamp:

```text
Roman: i | IV
Use: house, techno, modal groove, less tragic minor color
Variation: add bVII as a turnaround
```

Dark minor:

```text
Roman: i | iv | v | iv
Use: sparse techno, cinematic loops
Variation: use V instead of v for stronger pull
```

Pedal harmony:

```text
Bass: tonic pedal
Upper: i | bVII | bVI | bVII
Use: hypnotic loops with clear tonal center
```

## Bass Recipes

Root support:

- Play chord roots on bar starts.
- Add octave hits for motion.
- Best when harmony or melody is already busy.

Octave pulse:

- Alternate root and octave.
- Place octave on offbeats or late sixteenths.
- Best for house and techno momentum.

Syncopated answer:

- Kick states the downbeat.
- Bass answers on `1a`, `2&`, `3a`, or `4&`.
- Best for keeping the kick clear.

Kick-avoid composite:

- Write kick first.
- Reserve bass attacks for steps without kicks.
- Use short bass durations around kick hits.

## Melody Recipes

Motif:

- Use 3 to 5 notes.
- Repeat rhythm more than pitch.
- End phrase on tonic, fifth, or chord tone.

Call and response:

- Call in bars 1-2.
- Response in bars 3-4.
- Change ending note or rhythm, not everything.

Range expansion:

- Start within a fifth.
- Move up in build sections.
- Save highest note for hook or peak.

Rhythmic displacement:

- Repeat the motif one eighth or one sixteenth later.
- Keep pitch stable so the listener recognizes the idea.

Variation set:

- Original.
- Same rhythm, new ending.
- Same pitches, new rhythm.
- Shortened version for transitions.

## Variation Moves

Use one variation move at a time:

- Add or remove the last kick.
- Open the hat every second bar.
- Drop bass for one beat before a section.
- Invert chord voicing.
- Move melody up an octave for the peak.
- Replace the final melody note with a suspension.
- Silence foreground for one bar before the hook returns.
