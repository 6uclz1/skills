# Creative Strategies

Use these prompts to break creative stalls and finish tracks. They are practical adaptations of common electronic-music production strategies, including the user-provided summary of *Making Music: 74 Creative Strategies for Electronic Music Producers*.

## Table of Contents

- Beginning
- Progressing
- Finishing
- Diagnostic prompts

## Beginning

Use constraints before generating material:

- Make the track from one sample, one synth, one drum kit, or one scale.
- Ban one habit: no sidechain pump, no minor i-VI-III-VII, no four-on-the-floor, no supersaw, or no long reverb.
- Limit the sketch to 20 minutes.
- Use only 4 tracks: drums, bass, harmony, melody.

Separate generation and editing:

- Generation pass: write options, do not delete, do not judge.
- Editing pass: create nothing new, only select, cut, simplify, and arrange.

If the blank page is blocking progress, perfect one part first:

- Drum-only release-quality loop.
- Bass-only groove that survives without drums.
- Chord-only texture with strong voice leading.
- Melody-only hook with two variations.

## Progressing

Assign depth while composing:

- Foreground: hook, vocal-like lead, sharp transient, fast motion.
- Middle ground: chords, arps, stabs, rhythmic support.
- Background: bass, drones, pads, noise beds, slow motion.

Treat kick and bass as one composite low-end line:

- Mark every kick hit.
- Place bass notes between, after, or deliberately with kicks.
- Avoid sustained bass masking the kick transient.
- Use rests as part of the bassline.

Use asynchronous loops for motion:

- 4-step hat accent over 16-step drums.
- 5-step percussion pattern over a 4-bar loop.
- 3-note melody cell over a 4-chord loop.
- 7-step automation rhythm over 8 bars.

Use breadth before depth:

- Roughly sketch the whole loop before choosing perfect sounds.
- Write placeholder patches and replace later.
- Make arrangement decisions before micro-editing envelopes.

## Finishing

Arrange by subtraction:

- Fill the full timeline with the maximal loop.
- Remove parts to create sections.
- Add transitions only where subtraction leaves a gap.

Start from maximal density when stuck:

- Build the peak section first.
- Duplicate it across the arrangement.
- Mute parts to create intro, build, break, and outro.

Borrow a formal skeleton:

- Choose a reference track.
- Mark section lengths and energy changes.
- Remove the reference and map your material to the markers.

Commit by rendering:

- Bounce MIDI parts when the notes and sound are good enough.
- Delete or disable the source instrument when endless tweaking starts.
- Keep one intentional revision path, not unlimited options.

Finish failed sketches:

- Complete the arrangement even if the track is not strong.
- Export a rough master.
- Write one lesson learned.
- Start the next track with that lesson as a constraint.

## Diagnostic Prompts

Use these when the user is blocked:

- What decision are you avoiding: notes, sound, structure, or mix?
- Which part carries identity if all other parts are muted?
- What can be removed without hurting the hook?
- Is the low end one line or two conflicting lines?
- Where is the first moment of maximum density?
- What must be rendered to stop option-chasing?
