# Citation Policy

Place citations near the claims they support. Do not bury important citations in a generic source list.

## Cite Claims When They Involve

- Current facts
- Product capabilities
- Legal, medical, financial, security, or regulatory facts
- Vendor-specific behavior
- Statistics or benchmarks
- Direct quotes
- Controversial claims
- Comparisons between current products or services

## Citation Usually Not Needed

- Pure reasoning
- User-provided facts
- Common conceptual explanations
- Clearly marked assumptions
- Recommendations derived from already cited facts

## Citation Granularity

Bad:

> Skills are useful and can do many things. Source: ...

Good:

> Skills should be described as reusable task workflows and the final answer should cite the official source immediately next to that claim.

## Direct Quotes

- Quote only when wording matters.
- Keep excerpts short.
- Prefer paraphrase for general explanation.
- Never let a quote replace analysis.

## Claim Support Check

Before finalizing a cited claim, ask:

- Does the cited source directly support this exact claim?
- Is the source current enough for the claim?
- Is the source primary or otherwise strong enough?
- Did I distinguish source facts from my recommendation?
