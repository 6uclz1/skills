# Uncertainty Policy

Use explicit uncertainty instead of weak overconfidence.

## Separate

- Facts: directly supported by user-provided material or sources.
- Assumptions: needed to proceed but not confirmed.
- Inferences: conclusions drawn from facts and assumptions.
- Recommendations: advised actions based on the analysis.
- Unknowns: information that remains missing or unresolved.

## Confidence Labels

| Confidence | Meaning |
|---|---|
| High | Strong sources, consistent evidence, clear scope |
| Medium | Good evidence but some missing context, ambiguity, or version sensitivity |
| Low | Limited sources, conflicting signals, or important missing requirements |

## When Sources Conflict

1. Prefer primary sources over secondary sources.
2. Prefer newer official information when the version context matches.
3. Explain the conflict if it affects the answer.
4. State what would resolve the uncertainty.
5. Avoid forcing a false conclusion.

## Language

- Use "likely" when evidence supports a conclusion but does not prove it.
- Use "I would treat this as unresolved" when evidence conflicts materially.
- Use "under these assumptions" for conditional recommendations.
- Avoid absolute claims unless the evidence and scope justify them.
